## 20-30 Minute Reading Session Structure

### Every Day (5-10 mins each)

- **Word Recognition**: Pick one level, read all words
- **Theme Vocabulary**: Pick one theme, read all words
- **Sentence Reading**: Read simple to complex sentences
- **Copy Writing**: Copy 1-2 sentences

## When to Move to Different Levels

### Move FROM Level 1 TO Level 2 when:
- [ ] Can read all Level 1 words without help
- [ ] Can read 5+ Level 1 words per minute
- [ ] Feeling confident with Level 1

### Move FROM Level 2 TO Level 3 when:
- [ ] Can read all Level 2 words confidently
- [ ] Recognizing words instantly
- [ ] Comfortable with copying Level 2 words

### Move FROM Level 3 TO Level 4 when:
- [ ] Can read all Level 3 words
- [ ] Ready for longer, more complex sentences
- [ ] Writing speed improves

## Special Tips

### On Difficult Days
- Just read one section
- Pick the easiest file
- Read with a parent
- No pressure, just enjoy

### When Something Feels Too Hard
- Go back one level
- Spend extra time there
- No rush, learning is gradual
- Every child is unique

### When Moving Too Fast
- Add an extra day per level
- Spend more time on copy writing
- Focus on accuracy, not speed

### Celebrating Progress
- Mark completed weeks
- Tell family about achievements
- Reward consistent practice
- Every day is a win!

## Parent Guidelines

### Daily Practice Structure
- **20-30 minutes** is ideal length
- **Same time each day** helps habit formation
- **Read aloud** always
- **Celebrate effort** not just perfection

### What to Watch For
- ✓ Increased confidence
- ✓ Faster word recognition
- ✓ Better pronunciation
- ✓ More independent reading
- ✓ Enjoying the practice

### Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge**: Child doesn't want to read
**Solution**:
- Make it fun, not a chore
- Pick their favorite theme
- Read together
- Keep sessions short

**Challenge**: Child confuses similar words
**Solution**:
- Practice the confusing pair
- Use pictures/objects
- Real-life examples
- Extra repetition

**Challenge**: Copying is slow
**Solution**:
- Practice pen grip
- Start with 2-3 words
- Gradually increase
- Small words first

**Challenge**: Loses focus
**Solution**:
- Shorter sessions
- More breaks
- Change themes often
- Physical activity between sections
